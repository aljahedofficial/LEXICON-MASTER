VISUAL DESIGN PHILOSOPHY2.1 Design Aesthetic: "Modern Academic Luxury"

Overall tone: Professional, sophisticated, trustworthy, yet innovative
Feel: Like a high-end research dashboard meets modern SaaS application
Color Palette:

Primary: Deep Navy Blue (#1A2a4A) - Trust, professionalism, intelligence
Secondary: Vibrant Teal/Cyan (#00D9FF) - Modern, energetic, clarity
Accent: Warm Gold (#FFB84D) - Sophistication, highlighting key data
Success: Emerald Green (#10B981) - Positive actions, completion
Alert: Coral Red (#FF6B6B) - Warnings, errors (softer than standard red)
Neutral: Slate Gray (#64748B) - Secondary text, borders
Background: Off-white with subtle gradient (#F8FAFC to #F1F5F9)


2.2 Typography Strategy

Display Font: "Georgia" or "Playfair Display" - Elegant, authoritative headers
Body Font: "Segoe UI" or "Lato" - Clean, highly readable for tables and data
Monospace: "Fira Code" or "Monaco" - For word displays and technical elements
Font hierarchy: Clear distinction between H1 (28px), H2 (22px), H3 (18px), body (14-16px)
2.3 Visual Principles

Data visualization: Charts with smooth animations, hover interactions showing detailed tooltips
Micro-interactions: Buttons with subtle hover effects, smooth transitions on all interactive elements
Depth & Layering: Strategic use of shadows, glass-morphism cards, layered information density
Whitespace: Generous spacing between sections; breathing room for cognitive processing
Accessibility: High contrast ratios, clear iconography, alternative text for all visuals
